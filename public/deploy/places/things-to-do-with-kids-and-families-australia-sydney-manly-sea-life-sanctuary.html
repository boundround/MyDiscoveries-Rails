Overview

Swim with sharks, waddle like a penguin, tickle a starfish and learn about how we all the cool things to keep our marine life safe at Manly SEA LIFE Sanctuary. A short walk from Manly Wharf, families can spend a couple of hours learning and interacting with marine life through talks, tours and feeds. Peer into the eerily lit tanks and marvel at the strange shaped fish floating by.  Or spot one of the leafy sea dragons, often found in the waters outside the Sanctuary. Discover the secrets of Sydney Harbour in Underwater Sydney, home to octopus, seahorses, lionfish, sharks, cuttlefish and more. Hang around the Interactive touch pool where anyone can pick up and investigate its briny inhabitants – from sea slugs to sea stars, hermit crabs to sea urchins.  

Make sure to pass by Penguin Cove where Manly’s famous Little Penguins captivate visitors at all times of day.  Learn about the local colony of Little Penguins, one of the last colonies on the Australian mainland and efforts to protect this endangered population. Peer into the rehabilitation facilities and discover the conservation work taking place at The Sanctuary. See some of the success stories first hand with patients drawn from local species such as Little Penguins, Green Sea Turtles and Grey Nurse Sharks. The big draw card is Shark Harbour, a giant oceanarium accessed through an underwater viewing tunnel.  Nothing beats a fish eye view of Grey Nurse Sharks and Green Sea Turtles swimming overhead. Spot stingrays gliding above the glass and try keep up with schools of fish and other marine life as they circulate around the tank above. A fun experience for older children or teens is the Shark Feed Extreme or the Shark Dive Extreme – interactive opportunities to swim with Murdoch and Freckles, two juvenile captive bred Grey Nurse Sharks and the other residents of the oceanarium. There are daily tours and talks where visitors can learn the stories of some of the animals – from the sharks to penguins. Time your visit to certain days of the week and watch divers bravely hand-feeding the animals. 

Good to know

  • Manly SEA LIFE Sanctuary is a small but manageable attraction easily conquered in a couple of hours.
  • It’s ideally suited to small and younger children with the Shark Dive Experience a unique one-off tweens and teens.
  • There is a small cafe near the entrance to the Sanctuary and public toilets outside too.
  • Pack swimmers for a swim in neighbouring Manly Cove or a play on the sand.
  • Local parking is limited. There are Manly Council car parks nearby but all involve a short walk to The Sanctuary.
